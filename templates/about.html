About Us

We are a team passionate about solving real-world problems in manufacturing using the power of artificial intelligence. Our project, Enhancing Product Reliability, focuses on automating the detection of casting defects using deep learning.

The Problem

Casting is a widely used manufacturing process where molten material is poured into a mold to form a desired shape. However, this process is prone to defects such as blow holes, pinholes, burrs, shrinkage cavities, and metallurgical inconsistencies. These defects can compromise product quality and lead to costly rejections.

Most industries rely on manual inspection to detect these flaws. This process is slow, labor-intensive, and not always accurate. Even a small oversight can result in the rejection of an entire batch, causing significant financial loss.

Our Motivation

I am part of a team in the casting industry. I'm trying to ensure that every product we ship is free from defects, but our current inspection process is manual and inconsistent, because it relies on human judgment, which is time-consuming and prone to error — which makes me feel frustrated and worried about costly rejections and production delays.

Our Solution

To address this, we developed a deep learning model using transfer learning with the VGG16 architecture. This model is trained to classify casting products as either defective or good based on image data. By integrating this model into a web application, we provide a simple and efficient tool for automated quality inspection.

Our goal is to reduce inspection time, improve accuracy, and help manufacturers maintain high standards of product reliability — all while minimizing waste and cost.

Technology Stack

  • Python & Flask for backend development
  • TensorFlow and Keras for deep learning and transfer learning
  • VGG16 pre-trained model for image classification
  • Tailwind CSS for responsive and modern UI design

We believe that combining AI with practical manufacturing needs can lead to smarter, faster, and more reliable production processes.
